THE DRUID HILL PARK RESTORATION:

BALTIMORE GREEN SPACE

Overview

Druid Hill Park was founded in 1860 and is widely considered one of the oldest public parks in the United States. It is home to a variety of things for people to enjoy such as playgrounds, beautiful sceneries, tennis courts, and the Maryland Zoo. Its large, man-made lake is used to supply clean drinking water to residential neighborhoods downhill. This park’s past history of racism and segregation has recently pushed residents to demand the city to pass measures to increase access to the park. Most recently, however, the city has pushed for an FDA-mandated renovation of the reservoir area of the park.

The Construction Plan

The Druid Hill Park water tank construction is the first of its kind to be built within a reservoir. Construction began April 10, 2017, and is expected to be completed by January 2022. A significant chunk of the lake/reservoir will be filled in with dirt and turned into more grass and green space. To understand what the city wanted to change about this historical lake, we pursued an interview with one of the on-site managers. When we spoke to one of the members of the worksite, we were granted viewing access to the zoning plans and layouts that the general contractors were following. The on-site manager we spoke to described the project to be driven primarily by a state measure requiring an upgrade to uncovered water reservoirs.

“You’ll see... we took a huge stone, that was about 20 to 24 inches thick, and we pushed it all the way across, and it follows this path,” he points at the map on the table, “it’s just a big dam that we made, so we dammed it, put those curtains up to stop any kind of turbidity or anything from coming down here to where the effluent pipe is... we dug out this whole area.”

He describes the large disks that stand out within the construction site— these are the tops of the tanks being built within the reservoir, one of which is currently being filled in.

The formerly open-water reservoir serves nearby neighborhoods downhill from the area, including Hampden and Charles Village. He describes that the purpose of the water now being stored in tanks is “to keep it safer from the public,” citing an EPA-mandated measure for counter-terrorism. Although counter-terrorism is cited as one of the main purposes of the restoration, the on-site manager mentions other projects attached to it—

“for the most part, [the city] wanted some extra green space, recreational areas... It's a big upgrade to your living standard, especially cleaner, safer water, bigger living spaces, [and a] small amphitheater.”

Conclusion

Value of the Restoration: Is This What Druid Hill Needs?

While this restoration and renovation certainly keeps the people of Baltimore safer from potential terrorism, is this really all that the park needs? Druid Hill Park is used by so many people in so many different ways, and the ongoing restoration has made it challenging for people to enjoy all of its amenities. Additionally, the construction on-site has created a lot of temporary debris that is expected to be removed after construction— however, it is unclear how long this might take. There are parts of the park that could use more attention. Many of the benches are surrounded by trash with some even falling apart themselves. There are not many trash cans placed in often-used places around the park. Littering is a big problem on the bike/running path and, just a few steps over a small hill, there is a large pile even including things like a torn-apart refrigerator. All in all, this renovation is very important, however it feels incomplete. There is so much more to be done to help the people of Baltimore and those that use the park on a regular basis. Once more steps are taken towards the further improvement, this “renovation” can be considered a complete success.
